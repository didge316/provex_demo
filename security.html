How to keep your HEX safe

Do

If you plan to store more than $10,000 of crypto, you should get a hardware wallet. Keep in mind that even a small $1000 investment may easily grow into a $10,000 one. Hardware wallets are very easy to use, so don't be intimidated.

1) Buy a Trezor Model One (roughly $70).
2) Once your Trezor arrives follow the directions and set it up. Write down the 12 "seed" words (or "secret recovery phrase") it shows you by hand with a pen on a piece of paper. Store that piece of paper in a different location from your Trezor; such as a safe deposit box at your bank, or in the house of a friend or family member you trust.
3) Download the MetaMask Wallet browser extension.
4) Connect your Trezor to MetaMask Wallet.
5) Send a small amount of crypto to your Trezor (the PulseChain address shown within MetaMask Wallet).
6) Try sending the amount back to another wallet you own to test if your Trezor works and you set up everything correctly.
7) Learn more about how HEX works, keep an eye on your Miners, be responsible.

Don't

- Lose your secret recovery phrase (or "seed"), show it to anyone, make a photo of it, store it online or print it out. Never ever type those 12 words into any computer or give them to someone who's asking for them. If you need to restore your wallet you enter the 12 words on the Trezor itself. If a website asks for your seed words it is 100% a scam.
- Split the 12 words up. Write all of them in the right order on a single piece of paper.
- Write down something like "Backup for my $50,000 crypto investment" next to the 12 words. You'll remember what they are for without writing anything next to them.

- Brag about how rich you are. If you really need to, do it online and as anonymously as possible. Use an alias.

- Get greedy. Never try margin trading. You will lose all of your money.
- Answer random DMs from "admins" that try to help you. They are all scammers. There is nothing like a HEX/Pulse support/help desk.
- Spam, shill, be rude.

- Buy something you don't understand.
- Expect profits from the work of others. HEX is not a security, nobody will work for you. Nobody owes you anything.
- Emergency End Miner (EEM) if you don't really know what you are doing. You might get penalized and lose all of your HEX.

I own less than $1000 of crypto

In this case using plain MetaMask Wallet without a hardware wallet is sufficiently secure. Still write down your secret recovery phrase.

It's not worth it to spend $100 on a piece of hardware to protect less than $1000.
